Johannes Vermeer

Johannes Vermeer (1632 - 1675)

Johannes Vermeer, also known as Jan Vermeer or Jan Vermeer van Delft (October 31, 1632 – December 15, 1675), was a renowned Dutch Baroque painter of the 17th century (Golden Age of Dutch Painting). Vermeer is often referred to as a painter of women. His paintings feature 42 women and 13 men, which is four times more women compared to the usual ratio of men to women in European art of that period. Vermeer was known for his intimate depictions of female figures, often portraying women in everyday situations or in quiet moments of solitude. His portraits of women are notable for their sophistication, attention to detail, and play of light, contributing to his reputation as a master of capturing the intimacy and beauty of the female form.

Although only 36 paintings have survived to this day that can be confidently attributed to Vermeer, he likely painted more, but not exceeding 60. This is a modest number by the standards of the 17th century. For example, his famous contemporary Rembrandt left behind hundreds of paintings, along with countless engravings and drawings. Furthermore, most of Vermeer's paintings are small in size. If we were to gather them all together, they would almost fit within Rembrandt's "The Night Watch" (3.63 x 4.37 meters), which Rembrandt painted in a matter of months.
