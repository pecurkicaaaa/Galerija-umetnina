David

David

Michelangelo



Michelangelo's David is a masterpiece of the Renaissance era, sculpted in marble between 1501 and 1504. The statue, standing 5.17 meters tall including the base, represents the biblical figure of David in the moment of concentration before his battle with Goliath. It was sculpted as a symbol of the city-state of Florence during the medieval period. Originally, it was placed in front of the current Palazzo Vecchio, the seat of Florentine government. The sculpture was unveiled on September 8, 1504. A replica of the sculpture has been placed at the original location since 1910, while the original has been protected from damage and preserved since 1873 in the Galleria dell'Accademia in Florence. Michelangelo's heroic figure depicts a sixteen-year-old boy who has not yet fully matured into adulthood but possesses strong muscles developed from hard work at that age. A belt rests on his left shoulder, and the stone is still in his right hand, with his muscles tense and his forehead furrowed in a defiant expression.
